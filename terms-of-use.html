8.  Humane Research

SARMMIES ANALYTICAL LLC understands the importance of adhering to federal standards set forth by the United States government and other governing bodies regarding non-animal testing methods; in particular, we have always supported this organization's efforts in exploring alternatives to animal experiments. Using any kind of animal for scientific research has strict regulations that must be followed. All members and institutions are required to follow these guidelines, even those with proper state licenses.  We strongly encourage all research to be conducted in compliance with the appropriate regulatory framework.

  • The use of animals as a testing method in the United States must be designed, reviewed, and implemented following accepted ethical guidelines set forth by the federal government; mammalian research must meet high ethical and scientific standards. Before conducting any kind of study on mammalian research, scientists must follow guidelines set by the U.S Department of Health and Human Services (45 CFR 46), as well as abiding by rules established under Food & Drug Administration regulation among other guidelines (21 CFR 50/56). It is also the responsibility of each institution that oversees animal testing to ensure all individuals using animals for experiments or teaching have been appropriately trained.
  • This document will outline what SARMMIES ANALYTICAL LLC requires of its employees and collaborators. First, SARMMIES ANALYTICAL LLC shall provide pre-approval for all researchers, institutions, or independent companies before they can proceed with tests on animals. The following outlines additional criteria for obtaining approval:
    1)  All procedures are performed under anesthesia as prescribed by the Institutional Animal Care and Use Committee (IACUC) and/or Office of Laboratory Animal Welfare (OLAW) National Institutes of Health, 6700B Rockledge Drive, Suite 2500, MSC 6910 Bethesda, MD 20892.
    2)  The animal's pain and distress will be minimized in accordance with the Report of the Institute of Laboratory Animal Resources (1996)
    3)  The Principal Investigator (PI) will minimize duplication of research effort. If a procedure has already been approved within the past six months, it may not be submitted for additional approval unless significantly different from procedures previously reviewed. When duplicative submissions occur, SARMMIES ANALYTICAL LLC (if/when applicable) will forward all proposals to IACUC/OLAW for review.
    4)  No primates or endangered species are used.
    5)  All animals must be anesthetized before any blood draws or weight measurements are taken.
    6)  All animals must be housed in a temperature and humidity-controlled environments. The environment must be suitable for the species, age, health status, and breeding status of the animal(s).
    7)  All procedures will be performed as carefully as possible to avoid pain or distress to the animals. Procedures that may cause more than momentary pain or distress should be carried out with appropriate sedation/anesthesia unless it has been demonstrated that such procedures are not painful nor stressful to the animal(s).
    8)  No food or water deprivation shall exceed 24 hours except in special circumstances (e.g., experimental manipulations involving hunger cues, Ramadan fasting by Muslim employees). In these cases, the IACUC-approved plan for food deprivation shall state: one, how food deprivation will be measured; and two, the justification for food deprivation.
  • SARMMIES ANALYTICAL LLC understands that these federal standards were put into place to ensure that all research is conducted humanely and in compliance with the Animal Welfare Act (AWA), and we again advocate compliance with all applicable legal requirements.
